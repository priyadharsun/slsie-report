Section 01

Platform Overview & Architecture

SLSIE (Sri Lanka Sports Intelligence Ecosystem) is a web-based sports management and intelligence platform purpose-built for the National Olympic Committee of Sri Lanka (NOCSL). It consolidates federation governance, athlete health monitoring, gazette compliance enforcement, AI-powered analytics, and reporting into a single unified system accessible by authorised users from any device.

🏛️
34 National Sports Federations
All NSFs affiliated to NOCSL managed in one platform — governance scores, compliance status, EC officer registers, and athlete data.
🤖
Anthropic Claude AI Integration
Three AI capabilities — natural language chatbot, athlete coaching insights, and federation governance analysis — powered by Claude Haiku API.
⚖️
Gazette No. 2437/24 Compliance
National Sports Associations Regulations No. 01 of 2025 enforced automatically — grading, term limits, gender quotas, and 5-point compliance checklist.
📄
6 AI-Enhanced Reports
Governance, compliance, athlete risk, performance, Fit for Life, and donor impact reports — generated in seconds with AI executive summaries.
Technology Stack: Node.js v22 · Express.js · SQLite (node:sqlite built-in) · Anthropic Claude API · Bootstrap 5.3 · Chart.js 4.4 · SheetJS · Deployed on Render.com

Section 08

Fit for Life Programme

The Fit for Life module is NOCSL's digital management system for the IOC Fit for Life 2025–2035 community sports development programme. Unlike the elite athlete modules, this programme serves non-elite community participants — ordinary citizens across all nine provinces of Sri Lanka who participate in sport for health, wellbeing, and social development.

About IOC Fit for Life 2025–2035: The International Olympic Committee launched Fit for Life as its flagship community sports strategy under Olympic Agenda 2020+5. The programme's goal is to use sport as a tool for reducing non-communicable diseases, improving mental health outcomes, and building social cohesion in underserved communities. NOCSL, as the IOC-affiliated national committee, is the primary implementing body for Sri Lanka and is required to report programme outcomes to the IOC annually. SLSIE enables NOCSL to collect, manage, and report this data digitally for the first time.

Participant Health Data Collection Methodology

Each Fit for Life participant is registered in the system with a unique profile. Programme coordinators and coaches collect health data on a monthly basis through standardised assessments. Data is entered directly into SLSIE by provincial programme officers — eliminating paper forms and enabling real-time national reporting.

Donor & IOC Reporting

SLSIE generates a dedicated Donor Impact Report and a Fit for Life Programme Report directly from the data collected in this module. These reports replace weeks of manual data compilation that was previously required before each IOC review or donor progress meeting.

📄 IOC Annual Report Requirements
  • Total number of community participants by province, gender, and sport
  • Average health indicator improvements (BMI, wellbeing, sleep) from baseline to year-end
  • Total activity hours and session count — evidence of programme reach
  • Geographic coverage — provinces served and underserved region analysis
  • Trend data — year-on-year programme growth comparison
💰 UNDP / Donor Reporting Requirements
  • Number of direct beneficiaries reached — by demographic breakdown
  • Measurable health outcomes — percentage showing BMI improvement
  • Mental health impact — percentage reporting improved wellbeing scores
  • Budget utilisation evidence — cost per participant per outcome
  • SDG alignment evidence — linking programme outcomes to SDG 3 (Health) and SDG 10 (Inequality)

✅ Business Value

  • Demonstrates measurable programme reach and health impact to IOC, UNDP, and international donors with verified, real-time data
  • Provincial breakdown immediately shows geographic equity gaps — which regions are underserved and need additional investment
  • Monthly health indicator tracking shows whether the programme is delivering its core health objectives — not just participation numbers
  • Health outcome tracking supports NOCSL's mandatory reporting obligations under the IOC Fit for Life 2025–2035 framework
  • Donor Impact Report and Fit for Life Report are generated instantly from live data — eliminating 2–3 weeks of manual data compilation before each donor review
  • Digital evidence trail enables NOCSL to defend programme effectiveness to Ministry and justify continued funding requests
  • Wellbeing and sleep data go beyond standard physical health metrics — aligning with IOC's holistic athlete and participant welfare agenda

Section 12

Production System Architecture

The current SLSIE platform is a fully functional proof-of-concept that demonstrates the complete feature set. The production system — designed to serve all 34 federations, hundreds of athletes, and multiple concurrent government users reliably — requires a scaled, enterprise-grade architecture. This section describes how SLSIE would be built for full national deployment.

Design Principle: The production architecture is designed around three requirements — (1) reliability: the system must be available 99.9% of the time including during election periods, Ministry reviews, and international events; (2) security: athlete health data and federation governance data are sensitive and must meet Sri Lanka data protection standards; (3) scalability: the system must be able to grow from 34 federations to 100+ without re-architecture.
